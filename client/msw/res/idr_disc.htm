We Respect Your Privacy

Pueblo collects usage statistics so we can serve you better. Specifically, Pueblo tracks the following things:

  • What worlds you connect to.
  • The time that you connect to a world.
  • The time that you disconnect from a world.
  • The version of the Pueblo/UE client you are using.

That's it. We protect your privacy. Pueblo does not log your MUD account ids or anything else you type into a MUD.

We do not store any information that can be traced to you personally or to your computer. There is also no way to associate your registration details with the worlds you connect to. We will never sell or give away your personal details -- we hate spam just as much as you do!

Communications

You should be aware that world connections are almost always Telnet-based, which by its nature involves all communications being sent as plain text (without any encryption). This means that it is possible for third parties to snoop on your activities, and could even obtain your login id and password, since they are also sent in the clear. As stated above, we don't do this ourselves, but due to the nature of the Internet and its protocols we can't guarantee that you are secure -- and don't forget that other people connected to the world may be logging what you say when you're near them as a matter of course. So be careful with those trade secrets! ;-)

Passwords

If you elect to use Pueblo's automatic login feature, you should be aware that while Pueblo encrypts your password before saving it into the Personal World List (to discourage casual hackers), it is by no means a particularly secure algorithm (especially since the open-source nature of Pueblo makes the encryption and decryption routines publically accessible), and as noted above the password will be transmitted unencrypted to the world server. As with any other password, if you are worried about security you should change your passwords frequently.
